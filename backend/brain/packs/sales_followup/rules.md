\# Sales Follow‑Up Brain — Decision Rules



These rules govern how post‑meeting follow‑up messages are generated.

They are designed to improve clarity, intent, and response rates.



\---



\## Rule 1: Every Follow‑Up Must Have a Single Objective



A follow‑up message must pursue \*\*one\*\* of the following objectives:

\- Schedule a next meeting

\- Request specific information

\- Move toward a close

\- Re‑engage a stalled conversation



Multiple objectives in a single message are not allowed.



\---



\## Rule 2: Explicit Call‑to‑Action Is Mandatory



Every follow‑up must contain \*\*one clear and explicit CTA\*\*.

Examples:

\- “Can we schedule a 20‑minute call next week?”

\- “Please confirm whether Tuesday or Wednesday works.”



Vague CTAs such as “Let me know” are not acceptable.



\---



\## Rule 3: The Message Must Reference the Previous Interaction



The follow‑up must clearly reference:

\- The meeting, call, or discussion that already happened

\- A specific topic or point discussed



Generic follow‑ups without context are not permitted.



\---



\## Rule 4: Length Must Be Controlled



Follow‑ups must be:

\- Clear and concise

\- Free of unnecessary background

\- Short enough to be read in under 30 seconds



Over‑explaining reduces response probability.



\---



\## Rule 5: Tone Must Signal Professional Confidence



The message tone must be:

\- Professional

\- Polite

\- Confident



The following are prohibited:

\- Apologetic language

\- Desperation

\- Over‑politeness



\---



\## Rule 6: Only One Follow‑Up Thread at a Time



A follow‑up must not:

\- Introduce new topics

\- Combine unrelated discussions

\- Restart an old thread without acknowledgement



Each follow‑up continues exactly one conversation.



\---



\## Rule 7: Escalation Should Be Gradual



If previous follow‑ups received no response:

\- The message should become slightly more direct

\- The CTA should remain simple

\- The tone should remain respectful



Aggressive escalation is not allowed.
